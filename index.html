Three Branches of Governance

The Constitution

Constitution of the Republic of United Land

Preamble

We, the People of the Republic of United Land, in order to establish a sovereign and democratic state, to secure the blessings of liberty, justice, and prosperity for ourselves and future generations, to promote the common welfare and defend the dignity of every citizen, do ordain and establish this Constitution as the supreme governing instrument of our Republic.

Recognizing the inherent right of peoples to self-determination as affirmed by the principles of international law, and committed to the peaceful development of our nation through transparent governance and civic participation, we hereby declare the foundation of this constitutional order.

Article I

Sovereignty & Territory — The Republic of United Land is a sovereign, democratic republic. Sovereignty resides in the People, from whom all governmental authority derives.

Article II

Fundamental Rights — All citizens are guaranteed the rights to life, liberty, personal security, freedom of expression, peaceful assembly, and equal protection under the law.

Article III

Executive Authority — Executive power is vested in the President, who serves as Head of State and Head of Government, elected by universal suffrage for a term not exceeding five years.

Article IV

Legislative Power — All legislative authority is vested in the National Assembly, whose members are elected by proportional representation from among the citizenry.

Article V

Judicial Independence — The Judiciary is independent. Judges serve during good behavior and may not be removed except through constitutional process.

Article VI

Citizenship — Citizenship may be acquired by birth, naturalization, or special grant by the National Assembly. No citizen shall be deprived of citizenship without due process.

Article VII

Amendments — This Constitution may be amended by a two-thirds supermajority of the National Assembly, followed by ratification through national referendum.

Citizenship & Naturalization

Pathways to Citizenship

The Republic of United Land welcomes individuals who share our commitment to democratic governance, mutual respect, and community development. Citizenship confers the right to participate in governance, vote in elections, and access the full protections of our Constitution.

  • Formal application through the Ministry of Interior
  • Demonstrated commitment to founding principles
  • Completion of the Civic Knowledge Assessment
  • Oath of Allegiance to the Constitution
  • Approval by the Citizenship Review Board
  • No conflicting sovereign obligations
