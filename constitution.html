Constitution of the Republic of United Land

Ratified by the National Assembly
We, the People of the Republic of United Land, in order to establish a sovereign and democratic state, to secure the blessings of liberty, justice, and prosperity for ourselves and future generations, to promote the common welfare and defend the dignity of every citizen, and recognizing the inherent right of peoples to self-determination as affirmed by the principles of international law, do ordain and establish this Constitution as the supreme governing instrument of our Republic.
Article I
Sovereignty & Territory

§1. The Republic of United Land is a sovereign, independent, and democratic republic.

§2. Sovereignty resides in the People, from whom all governmental authority derives. No person or body may exercise authority not emanating from the People.

§3. The territory of the Republic shall be defined by the Declaration of Establishment and any subsequent territorial instruments ratified by the National Assembly.

§4. The Republic shall pursue peaceful relations with all nations and shall not engage in acts of aggression.

Article II
Fundamental Rights & Freedoms

§1. All persons within the jurisdiction of the Republic are entitled to the rights and freedoms set forth herein, without distinction of any kind.

§2. Every citizen has the right to life, liberty, and personal security.

§3. Freedom of thought, expression, peaceful assembly, and association shall not be abridged.

§4. All citizens are equal before the law and are entitled to equal protection of the law.

§5. No citizen shall be subjected to arbitrary arrest, detention, or deprivation of property without due process of law.

§6. The right to privacy, including privacy of correspondence and personal data, shall be protected.

Article III
Executive Authority

§1. Executive power is vested in the President of the Republic, who shall serve as Head of State and Head of Government.

§2. The President shall be elected by universal suffrage of the citizenry for a term not exceeding five (5) years, renewable once.

§3. The President shall appoint and may dismiss Ministers of the Council, subject to confirmation by the National Assembly.

§4. The President shall conduct foreign affairs, negotiate treaties, and represent the Republic in international relations.

§5. The President may issue Executive Orders that carry the force of law, provided they do not contravene this Constitution or Acts of the National Assembly.

Article IV
Legislative Power

§1. All legislative authority is vested in the National Assembly, a unicameral body composed of Representatives elected by proportional representation.

§2. Representatives shall be elected for terms of four (4) years by universal suffrage of the citizenry.

§3. The National Assembly shall have the power to enact laws, approve the national budget, ratify treaties, and exercise oversight of the Executive.

§4. Laws shall be passed by simple majority unless this Constitution requires otherwise.

§5. The National Assembly may conduct investigations, summon witnesses, and request documents from any branch of government.

Article V
Judicial Independence

§1. Judicial power is vested in the Supreme Court and such subordinate courts as the National Assembly may establish.

§2. The Judiciary is independent. Judges shall serve during good behavior and may not be removed except by impeachment for cause.

§3. The Supreme Court shall have the power of constitutional review and may declare any law or executive action unconstitutional.

§4. All persons are entitled to a fair and public hearing by an impartial tribunal.

Article VI
Citizenship

§1. Citizenship of the Republic may be acquired by birth to a citizen, by naturalization, or by special grant of the National Assembly.

§2. No citizen shall be deprived of citizenship without due process of law.

§3. The rights and obligations of citizens shall be defined by this Constitution and the laws of the Republic.

§4. Dual citizenship shall be permitted, provided it does not create a conflict of obligations detrimental to the Republic.

Article VII
Amendments & Final Provisions

§1. This Constitution may be amended by a two-thirds (⅔) supermajority vote of the National Assembly, followed by ratification through national referendum by simple majority.

§2. No amendment shall abridge the fundamental rights established in Article II.

§3. This Constitution shall be the supreme law of the Republic. Any law, regulation, or act inconsistent with this Constitution shall be void.

§4. This Constitution enters into force upon ratification by the founding citizens of the Republic.

So Ordained and Established

By the authority of the People of the Republic of United Land

President of the Republic
